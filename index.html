About the course

Your future in the making

Join a programme with unrivalled industry links and excellent graduate employment rates.
Whether you’re interested in social media, digital marketing, coding or graphics, you’re sure to find your career path on our eclectic course.


Course Breakdown

During the three year course, you will work on individual and group projects for each of the below units:

Year 1

Term 1
During this term, you will need to work collaboratively to design a shared online environment and pitch this to the unit leader (James Morris). Then, individually you will need to reflect on your performance and what elements you brought to the project.
Term 2
You will be expected to participate in discussions during lectures and group workshops, and to present your own ideas and examples during workshops. You will also have to carry out your own research by participating in various online forums, social networks and user-generated content sites. In groups, you will build and manage an online community.

Theme 1 - Magazine Mash-up
Ravensbourne is host to the annual MozFest, held by Mozilla and each year, every Web Media student is given a free ticket to the weekend of talks and workshops hosted by various tech companies. For this unit, you will need to report on the goings on at the festival in groups. You will build a website to post blogs, videos and images and interact with the other attendees through social media. For the second part of the unit, you will need to build another website for blog posts, videos and images for the TeenTech event. TeenTech is an event held in the Copperbox arena, Stratford and is for young people aged 11-16 to get them interested in jobs in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics). Your grade will depend on the quality of work you produce and how well you work as a team.

Theme 2 - Web Design Principles
In this project theme you will learn the principles of visual communication by researching and designing objects that communicate. In particular, you will learn how people make sense of visual perceptions, how people get information from the web (how they read the page), how to look at everyday objects in a way to make them appear to be something else. You will also learn the basics of HTML and CSS.
There are multiple mini-projects to complete for this part of the unit:

  1. Eyebombing: Finding faces in everyday places and photographing them
  2. TheUnionHack: Mashing up flags to create new ones, and designing your own flag
  3. OneTweet: Designing a vector poster of a famous tweet to learn about visual design principles
  4. CodeInYourPlate: Coding your favourite recipe into a webpage.

During this unit, you will choose an area within the web industry to conduct a research project. You will need to conduct primary and secondary research and produce a written report of 1000-1500 words in your choice of medium (web page or essay). You will need to use the harvard referencing system.

During this unit, you will gain a deeper knowledge of HTML and CSS and a little bit of Javascript. You will also learn about the importance of content strategies and targeted audiences.
What you need to do:
There are two mini projects that make up this unit, the details of both are below:

  1. Sharing-Is-Caring: For this project, you need to pick a cause you care about and create a one-page website advocating it, using the visual design techniques and audience targeting you learned about in the first term.
  2. Our-Space: For this group project, you will need to create a website for the Web Media course to entice prospective students (can you guess what unit we’re doing?)

Year 2

This unit focuses on a variety of e-commerce platforms and gives students the opportunity to develop their entrepreneurial skills. Throughout its duration, Web Media students will collaborate with those from Digital Photography to develop e-commerce sites to market and sell photographic images.

During this unit you will learn how to mash-up Web technologies to make tools that everyone can use. By the end of this project, students will have become more familiar with programming, coding and research.

This unit introduces students to the methods and tools used to effectively address the needs and circumstances of Web users. You will learn to design and prototype Web products that are comfortable and even joyful to use.

Interactive Narratives will give students the chance to tell a story across different platforms, online games in particular. In the first half of this unit, students will create a narrative, or rework an existing one, as an interactive experience, which will also serve as the main “backstory” for the online game. In the second half, this backstory then serve as the foundations for the creation of a game or series of mini games.

Year 3

This unit focuses on new and advancing web technologies, focusing on big data and audio-visual enhancements.
In small groups, you will produce a pitch for a new service or adaptation of an existing website, blog or social media area to incorporate ‘intelligent’ content for commercial or social benefit. This will illustrate a personal understanding of the range of tools and techniques which offer some level of ‘intelligence’ to users and producers, and could include RSS feeds, tracking, monitoring of user behaviours, intelligent search, conversational agents or user interfaces, tag clouds, integration with iPhone applications, micro-payments, and so on.

In this unit, you will produce and pitch an advertising/marketing campaign for your portfolio that showcases your ideas, intelligence, communication skills and web media skills. Think and work to impress a potential employer!

The Major Project is the primary portfolio work a student performs to showcase their talent for the world of employment. This is where you show the world what you can do – or even create the foundations of the startup company that you will build into a successful business after graduation.
Students have the choice of either leading their own practical project OR producing a detailed business plan for a business start-up OR specializing in a particular skill area of Web Media.

Nice to Know

Our building is one of the most eye-catching in the country, a vibrant, exciting place to study and make friends.
Our Student Union at Ravensbourne is here to give you a memorable academic and social experience. RaveSU, based on the ground floor of the building provides students with any problems they may have, with lecturers, workloads, travel, finance extra. They also arrange socials and events for all students to attend, and host societies and clubs.
With 23% of your time spent in lectures, there’s plenty of time to work, study and socialise while learning.

James
Moris

“I am Course Leader of BA (Hons) Web Media Production. I have worked in the past as a technology journalist, including spending five years as editor of the UK's leading computing magazine, PC Pro. I also run a successful commercial video and online digital content production company, t-zero communications Ltd.
Most recently I’ve been working on projects with the Royal Shakespeare Company and London Symphony Orchestra. For the RSC, I managed the Ravensbourne end of a collaboration where Shakespeare plays are streamed into schools, with the performance of Richard II reaching over 30,000 school students. My book "Can Computers Create Art?" was published by Atropos Press in 2009. Follow me @Cyberwest."

Matteo
Menapace

“I have been creating with computers since I was 3, using my dad’s work machine to make abstract drawings. And that’s what I still do, more or less: making abstract things with code.
Between then and now, I trained as a communication designer, taught myself how to program computers and made educational games for clients like the BBC.
Currently I teach people aged 7 to 60+ how to be creative with code.
You can read about my teaching philosophy at github.com/matteomenapace/tm and follow me at @baddeo.”

Tim
Riley

I spent the first 23 years of my career as a motion graphics designer and graphics manager. During this time I developed diverse creative projects for the broadcast and media industries and latterly the internet in the areas of branding, title sequences, promotions, creative services and pop promos.
Over the last ten years I gained an extensive knowledge of the internet, web and digital media through a PhD from University of Westminster, which explored the sharing of self-created digital content through social media, and a Masters in Digital Media.
I’ve been lecturing at Ravensbourne for over 10 years and enjoy the vibrant and creative atmosphere. Find me online at timrileydigital.com or follow me @timfrantic.
